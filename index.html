My story till now:

  • I was born in May 1993 in Chandigarh, India
  • My undergraduate is in Electrical Engineering. However, on most days, I forget what RLC circuits were all about. Engineering was mostly about doing theatre, and building long lasting friendships, and I'm glad I invested my time in those pursuits
  • In my first job as an analyst in McKinsey & Company, I advised multi billion dollar companies on how they could save money in IT functions
  • My favorite part of the job at McKinsey was working on Agile, Cloud, and Effectiveness of IT surveys. These surveys helped me see see that a lot of money could be saved by making operations efficient (through automation) and overcoming unnecessary inertia
  • Tired of the nothing good can happen in this country narrative, I decided I had to see how things worked on ground, and was fortunate enough to work in India's largest NGO, Pratham Education Foundation, for around 2.5 years
  • In Pratham, I led the data collection, analysis and reporting of an in-school and in-community intervention operating across 14 Indian states
  • Throughout both of my jobs, I was fortunate to have worked on user surveys, and data collection and reporting products. These experiences, across two very different environments, helped me understand the reasons behind the adoption of technology, and how it can benefit organisations and lives
